The Asian Technical-Vocational Education Resource Center (ATVERC) is a project of the National Institute for Technical Education and Skills Development (NITESD) of the Technical Education and Skills Development Authority which aims to revive and rejuvenate the operation of Learning Resource Centers (LRC) in the Philippines and provide services as seen in the "Library of Commons".

Researchers and students may come to the ATVERC to explore the facility and gain access to resources with ease using in-house computer workstations or personal gadgets such as tablets and smartphones.

Background:

The Learning Resource Center concept was introduced in 2003 by the National Institute for Technical Vocational Education and Training (NITVET) as a functional area of the Competency-Based Training approach adopted by TESDA. This became part of the TESD Project under the Courseware Development sub-component and was mainly used to provide training support materials to various technical/vocational institutions. The central office through the TESD Project continually provided regional LRCs with support materials i.e., reference books, training manuals, video/audio tapes and staff training on LRC Management. In the year 2010, the LRC distributed the final batch of reference materials to identified Centers of Technical Excellence (CenTEx) composed of some TESDA training centers and selected TESDA-supervised schools.

LRC then was operated as a unit of the Curriculum and Training Aid Division (CTAD) with three staff including a licensed librarian. It functioned as a special library that supported book reading, video viewing, audio listening, and computer services for research work, internet, and interactive courseware training. The staff organized the collections making sure that everything is in place plus additional tasks of updating the electronic database, publishing lists of current collections and maintaining archives of agency publications.
Vision
To transform the facility into an E-Resource hub of TESDA.
Mission
Provide TVET with e-resources on technology, environment, teaching and learning strategies.
Objectives:
- Prepare the necessary facilities to build-up and store the data of computer-generated learning resources;
- Develop an interface where resources are displayed, accessed and maintained;
- Link databases of public and private TVIs;
- Establish a link at the TESDA website for e-resource sharing; and
- Promote utilization through advocacy and social media.

Expected Output:
- Facility containing the existing collections and data imaging system to convert selected TESDA documents to e-format;
- A public computer terminal to display e-resources and databases of LRC, public and private TVIs including web links (Webliography) of learning materials;
- Website featuring e-resources, databases, and web links.
DESCRIPTION OF THE PROJECT

The Asian Technical Vocational Education Resource Center shall serve as the hub of TVET e-resources in the country. Most of the content will come from online resources identified as weblinks and the rest are shared training resources from the TESDA training institutions.

Existing TESDA publications will be selected, digitized and organized into a database. Selection will be based on the criteria of usefulness to researchers such as reports, plans, researches, studies and other publications.



 Strategies:
- Conduct a physical inventory of resources and review user’s requirements;
- Maintain the database and update its contents;
- Maintain a subscription of technical and scientific journals including electronic copies;
- Maintain entries to webliography, review and remove expired links;
- Provide walk-in access to Learning Resource Center for the benefit of researchers;
- Participate in free trial subscriptions of titles of interest on TVET programs;
- Seek advice from users thru evaluation survey on adequacy of resources and user’s experience on e-resource services.
PROGRAM BENEFICIARES
The primary beneficiaries of the Center are the following:
- TVET students, trainers, officials and staff;
- Schools/Universities and Other Training Institutions; and
- Companies, NGOs, GOs, and LGUs
FEATURES OF THE PROJECT
The project will feature a facility containing computer-generated learning resources and may be accessed through the eResource area computer workstations. A link to the ATVERC will be made available in the TESDA website.


Clientele:

The Center shall cater to a very diverse set of clientele due to its wide range of services. These are:

- TVET Trainees
- TVET Trainers
- Technology Researchers
- Technology Students
- Industry Technologists
- Business Leaders and Entrepreneurs
- Project Enthusiasts
- Hobbyists
- School/Training Center Administrators
- Curriculum Developers
- General Public
